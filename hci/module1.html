Module 1 — Foundations of Human–Computer Interaction

Overview

HCI is the multidisciplinary field concerned with designing, evaluating, and implementing interactive computing systems for human use. It spans devices (desktop, mobile, wearables), looks at history (CLI → GUI → NUI), and combines computer science, psychology, design, ergonomics, and sociology.

Learning Objectives
  • Define HCI and explain its importance.
  • Identify contributing disciplines and the evolution of UIs.
  • Describe interactive system components and recognize good vs poor design.
1.1 What is HCI?

Definition and key quotes: “the discipline concerned with the design, evaluation, and implementation of interactive computing systems for human use” (Dix et al.).

1.2 Why is HCI Important?

Goals: make systems usable, useful, and desirable. Good HCI increases satisfaction, efficiency, accessibility; poor HCI causes frustration, errors, and exclusion.

1.3 History & 1.4 Multidisciplinary Roots

From CLIs to GUIs, to mobile and NUIs. Disciplines: CS, psychology, design, ergonomics, sociology, linguistics.

1.5 Components of Interaction

User, Goal, Interface (input/output), Context. The goal of HCI is aligning these components effectively.
