Module 9 — Accessibility and Inclusive Design

Overview

This module introduces accessibility and inclusive design: making systems usable by people with diverse abilities, and applying WCAG and assistive technology principles.

9.1 What is Accessibility?

Designing interfaces usable by people with a range of abilities (visual, auditory, motor, cognitive). Examples: screen readers, keyboard navigation, captions.

9.2 Inclusive Design

Inclusive design considers diversity (age, culture, ability). Principles: recognize exclusion, solve for one extend to many, learn from diversity.

9.5 WCAG & POUR Principles

WCAG principles: Perceivable, Operable, Understandable, Robust. Levels: A, AA (recommended), AAA.

9.6 Designing for Accessibility (Best Practices)
  • Provide alt text and text alternatives
  • Ensure color contrast, keyboard navigation, clear layouts
  • Provide captions and transcripts for media
  • Avoid conveying info by color alone
9.9 Laws & Standards

Examples: ADA, Section 508, EU Web Accessibility Directive, national policies (e.g., Philippine Web Accessibility Policy).
