Module 8 — Evaluation and Usability Testing

Overview

This module covers formative vs summative evaluation, usability attributes, testing methods, interpreting data, and the role of iteration in improving designs.

8.1 What is Evaluation?

Evaluation assesses usability, effectiveness, and satisfaction of a system. It identifies problems and guides improvements at any stage of development.

8.2 Types of Evaluation

Formative (during design to improve) and Summative (after to measure final performance).

8.4 Usability Testing

Plan → Prepare materials → Conduct (think-aloud encouraged) → Analyze results → Report recommendations. Collect quantitative (time, errors) and qualitative (comments) data.

8.6 Evaluation Methods

Methods include heuristic evaluation, cognitive walkthrough, think-aloud, surveys, field observation.
